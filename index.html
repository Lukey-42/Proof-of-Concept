Website Development Process

Introduction

Before diving headfirst into developing a website, it is vital that there is a plan for each phase of the process. Without one, the path will be long, messy, and disordered which will likely lead to a below standard website that doesn’t have what it needs to become mainstream. With these specific steps of the process laid out in an organized manner, there is some guidance and a solid plan, which I believe will help push companies further in their pursuit of success.

Organized Steps

1. Misinformation and Disinformation
The first part of the process of developing a website starts with the ethical consideration of the information that is being presented to the user. Whether it’s online shopping, news articles, or a social media platform, the company always needs to be aware of what they are telling people. If they are aware that they are spreading false information, that is considered disinformation and is common with so much data online in these modern times. While it’s important for users to look out for this kind of stuff, it is also the job of companies to ensure that the information they are using is backed by research and unbiased sources. This is the first step because it is what the website is being made for. If it is all just false information, then it’s setting itself up for failure from the beginning.

2. Content and IA
By prioritizing the content and infrastructure architecture, the foundation for the website is set. Understanding the purpose allows for everything else to be built around it to complete whatever objectives are set from the beginning. These are basically the blueprints for the entire website that provide a good starting point. The content is what brings the users to the website, and it needs to be the focus, otherwise people will have no reason to visit the site in the first place. And without information architecture which brings the content into a comprehensible format, every other step will be messy and aimless, leading to an incomplete final project.

3. Systems Design, Engineering and Organization
Once the main idea is understood and the general layout is planned, the next step in the process would be to design and engineer the website, bringing it to reality. A good system design allows for scalability for future growth in the company, security to preserve critical information on the company’s side and the user’s side and has an organized structure that provides easy-to-follow navigation. Having aspects like these requires many different elements in the design that require foresight from the beginning, which is why having the content and information structure planned before the design begins is crucial to a successful website.

4. UX and Visual Design
This is where the user becomes the main focus, with the designers ensuring that the website caters to people’s needs and keeps them coming back for more. The user experience is the interaction between people and the various features, whether it’s purchasing a book online or reaching out to the company through the “Contact Us” page. And the visual design is how the developers make the website attractive, bringing in customers through specifically chosen colors or aesthetically pleasing pictures. With the different features and elements playing a role in affecting how we behave, the company can keep people on their site for longer. This step is what brings the project together and is the most vital part of creating a popular website.

Conclusion

In the developing process of a website, it is important to focus on the steps at hand while also having a solid plan for the future of the company’s website. The steps I laid out to be followed provide a guide that gives developers an outline on how the process should be approached. I believe that by staying on track and building from the ground up, the results will be an organized and well-structured project that will attract users and keep them coming back.
